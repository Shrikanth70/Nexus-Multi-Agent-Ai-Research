# 🎨 UI Guidelines
## Nexus Research Intelligence Platform

> **Status:** Discovery
> **Phase:** 3 - Development Standards

The UI for Nexus must reflect a professional, enterprise-grade research tool. It is designed to be pristine, calm, and highly readable. 

*We are heavily inspired by Notion, Linear, and Perplexity.*

---

## 1. The "Bright Theme Only" Rule

**Nexus operates strictly in a Bright/Light Theme.** 
* **Why?** It enforces a focus on typography, spacing, and structural clarity. It conveys the feeling of a clean sheet of paper or a professional laboratory.
* **Banned Aesthetics:** Dark mode, neon colors, heavy glassmorphism, glowing borders.

## 2. Color Palette

* **Backgrounds:** Pure white (`#FFFFFF`) or extremely subtle off-white (`#FAFAFA`) for secondary panels.
* **Text:** Deep charcoal (`#1A1A1A`) for primary text. Slate gray (`#666666`) for secondary text.
* **Accents:** A single, muted primary color (e.g., a soft indigo or slate blue) used *very sparingly* for active states or primary buttons.
* **Borders:** Barely visible grays (`#EAEAEA`) for separation.

## 3. Typography

* **Font Family:** Inter, Roboto, or a similarly clean, geometric sans-serif.
* **Hierarchy:** Clear distinction between H1, H2, and body text. 
* **Readability:** High contrast and generous line height (`1.5` or `1.6` for long-form research text).

## 4. Animation and Layout

* **Animations:** Must be purposeful and calm. Simple fades or subtle slide-ins. No bouncing or heavy spring physics.
* **Layouts:** Use a sidebar navigation model. Main content areas should have constrained max-widths (e.g., `800px` to `1000px`) to prevent eye fatigue when reading long research reports.
* **Whitespace:** Generous padding and margins. Let the content breathe.
